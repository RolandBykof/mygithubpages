§ 1

Installation

The extension is a single JavaScript file (content.js) loaded as a Chrome or Edge browser extension using Developer Mode.

  1. Open your browser and navigate to the Extensions page (chrome://extensions in Chrome, edge://extensions in Edge).
  2. Enable Developer mode using the toggle in the top-right corner of the Extensions page.
  3. Click Load unpacked and select the folder that contains manifest.json and content.js.
  4. The extension appears in your list. Navigate to play.intobridge.com — the extension activates automatically on every game table page.
  5. After loading, open the browser console (F12 → Console tab) to confirm the message "IntoBridge Accessibility Extension V1.17 Loaded".
After updating content.js, click the refresh icon on the Extensions page and then reload the IntoBridge tab.
§ 2

Keyboard Modes

The extension operates in two distinct keyboard modes. Toggle between them at any time by pressing Z. The active mode is announced by the screen reader when you switch.

Cards Default mode

Bare letter keys browse your hand and the dummy. All query commands are available without holding Alt. Bidding and card-playing inputs are blocked to prevent accidental actions.

Keyboard Action mode

Bare letter keys submit bids and play cards. Query commands without Alt are blocked. Switch here when it is your turn to bid or play.

Regardless of the active mode, all Alt+letter query commands always work — you never need to switch mode just to check your hand.

Press Z → "Keyboard mode."
Press Z again → "Cards mode."
§ 3

Bidding

Bidding uses two keystrokes: first the level (1–7), then the strain. Switch to Keyboard mode before bidding.

  1. Press a digit 1–7. The screen reader confirms "Level 3?"
  2. Press the strain key. The bid is submitted immediately.
3 → "Level 3?"   N → "Bid: 3 NT."

Special calls require only a single key:

KeyAction
PPass
XDouble — or Redouble if a double is already on the table
EscapeCancel a partially entered bid
If you press the wrong level, simply press another digit before the strain key — the level is updated. You have 5 seconds to complete each two-key entry; after that it times out automatically.
§ 4

Playing Cards

Card play also uses two keystrokes: first the suit, then the rank. Switch to Keyboard mode before playing. The extension automatically plays from the correct hand — your own hand or the dummy's if you are declarer.

  1. Press the suit key. The screen reader confirms "Spade?"
  2. Press the rank key. The card is played immediately.
S → "Spade?"   A → "Spade A played from hand."

Quick-play: Arrow keys

When a trick is already in progress (1–3 cards on the table), the arrow keys play the lowest or highest card of the led suit from the active hand — without needing to specify the suit manually.

KeyAction
↓ Arrow DownPlay the lowest card of the led suit
↑ Arrow UpPlay the highest card of the led suit
Arrow keys are blocked when the table is empty (you would be leading) or when all four cards are already played. If you are void in the led suit, the extension tells you and you must choose a discard manually with the suit + rank keys.

Suit-following enforcement

The extension enforces bridge rules automatically. If you select a suit you are not void in while another suit was led, you receive a warning: "Spade? Warning: must follow Heart." If you confirm the illegal play, it is blocked with the message "Must follow suit. You have Heart."

§ 5

Query Commands

Query commands read information aloud via the screen reader. They are available in two ways:

  • In Cards mode — press the letter key alone (no Alt required).
  • In Keyboard mode — hold Alt and press the letter key.

This means you can always check your hand or the board without switching modes.

Cards  Press A → "3 Spades: A K Q"
Keyboard  Press Alt+A → "3 Spades: A K Q"

§ 7

Getting Started with Robots

If this is your first time using the extension, the quickest way to try it out is to play a solo practice game against three robots provided by IntoBridge. No other players are needed.

  1. Open https://play.intobridge.com/login and sign in to your IntoBridge account. If you do not have an account yet, register for free on the same page.
  2. On the lobby page, locate and activate the button labelled "Just Play — Jump in and play stress-free with three friendly robots". This immediately starts a full bridge game at a private table where your three opponents are computer-controlled robots.
  3. The extension activates automatically. After a couple of seconds you will hear the board announcement — for example "Board 1. None vulnerable." — confirming that the extension is running.
  4. The game begins in the bidding phase. Press Z to switch to Keyboard mode, then use the two-key bidding commands described in § 3 to make your bids.
  5. When play begins, explore all card-play and query commands in a low-stakes environment before playing against other people.
The robots play reasonable bridge and follow all standard rules, making them ideal for testing accessibility features without time pressure.
